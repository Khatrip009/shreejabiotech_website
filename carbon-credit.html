Frequently Asked Questions

What are carbon credits? ‚ñº

Carbon credits are measurable, verifiable permits that allow the owner to emit one tonne of carbon dioxide equivalent ($CO_{2}e$) from the atmosphere. They are generated by projects that reduce, remove, or avoid greenhouse gas emissions.

How do Bio-CNG plants generate carbon credits? ‚ñº

Bio-CNG plants generate carbon credits by preventing methane emissions from organic waste (which is a potent greenhouse gas) and by displacing fossil fuels with renewable Bio-CNG. Additionally, CO‚ÇÇ capture and organic fertilizer production further enhance emission reductions.

What is the difference between VCU and Gold Standard? ‚ñº

VCU (Verified Carbon Unit) is a type of carbon credit issued by Verra's Verified Carbon Standard (VCS) program, focusing primarily on emission reductions. Gold Standard is another robust certification body that also emphasizes sustainable development benefits alongside emission reductions, often commanding a premium price.

How long does the carbon credit registration process take? ‚ñº

The timeline varies depending on the project's complexity and the chosen standard, but typically ranges from 6 to 18 months, including project design, validation, monitoring, and verification phases. Shreeja Biotech streamlines this process for you.
